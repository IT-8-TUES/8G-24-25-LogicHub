MaxSun to launch Arc Pro B60 Dual Turbo, two GPUs, 48GB memory

Definitely not for gamer.

Many dismissed our report about a dual Battlemage GPU as impossible, but here it is. The card exists, though it’s not a typical gaming GPU. In fact, it may not function the same way at all.

MaxSun has shared the full specifications of its new card called the Arc Pro B60 Dual Turbo. As the name suggests, it’s a dual-GPU solution featuring two Battlemage BGM-G21 GPUs, each with 20 Xe2 cores. Importantly, each GPU has its own memory. Based on the information provided, this isn’t a traditional single graphics card like the GTX 690. Instead, it’s two GPUs integrated on a single board.

Source: MaxSun

The company explains that this graphics card will function as long as the motherboard supports PCIe x16 channel splitting (x8 + x8). The Arc Pro B60 is already limited to PCIe 5.0 x8 per GPU. Both GPUs share the same slot, but technically, they operate as two separate units. Anyway, remember that Battlemage was supposed to work with PCIe 5.0 specs? Well, this confirms that it does.

MaxSun confirms that the card is aimed entirely at AI developers and not gamers. It features 48GB of memory, 24GB per GPU, and supports large-scale models like DeepSeek R 70B and QwQ 32B.

Interestingly, the card uses a blower-style cooler, a large-area vapor chamber, and a metal backplate for heat dissipation. While the official announcement didn’t include power details, a datasheet we obtained confirms the card requires 400W. Since each Pro B60 GPU is rated for 200W, this setup avoids power bottlenecks.
