Noctua AIO coolers announced: no name yet, available in Q1 2026

With their brand-new AIO for CPUs, Noctua is finally making their long-overdue debut in the liquid-cooled industry.

Source: ComputerBase/HardwareCanucks

At Computex 2025, Noctua is showcasing its first all-in-one (AIO) liquid cooler, featuring the Asetek G8 V2 pump. Rather than developing its own pump, Noctua is focusing on optimizing cooling performance, efficiency, and noise levels through a series of advanced design choices.

The company has introduced its own sound isolation system for the pump unit. According to ComputerBase, “In addition to three-layer sound insulation, an integrated mass damper is designed to reduce pump vibrations.” The pump will support three operating modes: Quiet, Balanced, and Manual.

Source: ComputerBase

The radiator will be paired with either NF-A14x25 G2 (140 mm) or NF-A12x25 G2 (120 mm) fans. Noctua plans to release three size variants: 420 mm, 360 mm, and 240 mm.

These AIO coolers uses Noctua’s SecuFirm+ mounting system. As pointed out by other media, this could mean that users with existing Noctua air coolers that use the same mounting hardware may not need to replace the brackets, making installation simpler.

Source: ComputerBase

Noctua’s AIO coolers are scheduled to launch in the first quarter of next year, though the company has not provided an exact release date.

Source: HardwareCanucks, ComputerBase
