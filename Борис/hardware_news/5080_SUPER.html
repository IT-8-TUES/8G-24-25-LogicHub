RTX 5080 SUPER specifications revealed by Kopite7kimi

One of the most recognized NVIDIA leakers is back with an update on company plans.

Usually, when Kopite7kimi shares updates on the GeForce series, it means NVIDIA has provided specs to board partners. This doesn’t always mean the specs are final, and we’ve seen them change many times before.

Apparently, the time for the RTX 5080 SUPER has come. The leaker claims this model will feature the GB203-450 GPU with 10,752 CUDA cores. This rumor suggests that the RTX 5080 SUPER and RTX 5080 would share the same core count, which is surprising. However, the SUPER variant is said to include an upgrade in memory, now offering 24GB of GDDR7.

Of course, this means the RTX 5080 SUPER will use 3GB GDDR7 modules, just like the RTX 5090 (Laptop GPU). The leak goes further, claiming the RTX 5080 SUPER will also feature 32 Gbps memory—the fastest GDDR7 spec to date.

This would push bandwidth beyond the RTX 5080’s 960 GB/s, now exceeding 1024 GB/s (maximum theoretical value). Naturally, the increased memory capacity and speed would also raise power requirements, now said to be over 400W—at least 40W higher than the RTX 5080.

5080 SUPER 24GB, Source: MSI

Kopite did not mention when this card might be released, nor did he confirm the recent rumors about the RTX 5070 SUPER, which was also said to have 18GB of memory. The RTX 50 SUPER refresh wasn’t expected until Q4 this year, but it appears NVIDIA may be accelerating its plans.
